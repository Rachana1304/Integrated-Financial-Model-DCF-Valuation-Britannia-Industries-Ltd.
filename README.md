# Integrated Financial Model & DCF Valuation – Britannia Industries Ltd.

## Project Summary

Developed an integrated three-statement financial model for Britannia Industries Ltd. using six years of historical financial data (FY2020–FY2025). Forecasted financial performance through FY2030 based on historical trends and business assumptions, and performed a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation to estimate the company's intrinsic value and assess whether the stock was undervalued or overvalued.

## Business Objective

To estimate the intrinsic value of Britannia Industries Ltd. by building an integrated three-statement financial model and performing a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation based on projected future cash flows.

## Methodology

### Historical Analysis

- Collected and analyzed six years of financial statements.
- Identified historical trends in revenue, profitability, working capital, and capital expenditure.
- Evaluated key financial drivers to support forecasting assumptions.

### Forecasting

Projected financial performance from FY2026 to FY2030 using historical trends and business assumptions for:

- Revenue Growth
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
- EBITDA Margin
- EBIT Margin
- Tax Rate
- Working Capital
- Capital Expenditure (CapEx)
- Depreciation
- Debt
- Retained Earnings

### Integrated Financial Model

Developed a fully linked three-statement financial model where:

- Income Statement drives profitability.
- Balance Sheet reflects asset, liability, and equity movements.
- Cash Flow Statement calculates Free Cash Flow.
- All statements are dynamically linked using Excel formulas.

### Valuation

Performed a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation by calculating:

- Free Cash Flow (FCF)
- Discount Factors
- Present Value of Forecast Cash Flows
- Terminal Value
- Enterprise Value
- Equity Value
- Intrinsic Share Price

Compared the estimated intrinsic share price with the prevailing market price to determine whether the stock appeared undervalued or overvalued under the assumptions used.
